# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Iris and Niko, two people on a Central European trip (Prague, Vienna, Budapest). Using this exclusively on iPhones, installed as a PWA on the home screen. Opening it in the moment: at a café table, on the metro, standing outside a museum. They are not power users looking for features; they want orientation fast and then want to be present in the place they are standing in.

## Product Purpose

A personal offline-capable travel reference and memory capture tool for one specific trip. Provides an interactive itinerary, city activity guides, local language phrases, a currency calculator, and photo-based memory capture. Success looks like: opened mid-trip, understood in seconds, closed again. The app handles logistics quietly so the people using it can be present.

## Brand Personality

Warm, personal, grounded. This was made for two specific people for one specific trip, not for a market. It should feel like a hand-crafted travel journal, not a product. Not precious, not polished to abstraction — warm and real.

## Anti-references

- Generic travel apps (TripAdvisor, Google Trips): information-dense, impersonal, feels like a directory
- Dark utility UIs (Citymapper dark mode): high contrast, data-forward, cold
- "AI-generated" aesthetic: purple gradients, hero metrics, glassmorphism, left-stripe card accents
- Anything that looks like it could belong to any trip by any person

## Design Principles

1. **Warmth over precision.** Generous spacing, rounded forms, warm tones. The app should feel held, not clinical.
2. **One thing at a time.** Every screen has one focal point. Secondary information recedes naturally. No competing for attention.
3. **Personal, not generic.** City names, day labels, and place-specific details should feel handpicked. The app knows whose trip this is.
4. **Invisible infrastructure.** Offline capability, loading states, and data persistence are seamless. They never interrupt the experience or remind the user they are using software.
5. **Motion with purpose.** Transitions and micro-interactions feel native to iOS and unhurried. Nothing bounces. Nothing flashes.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as baseline. Primary users have no known accessibility needs, but the app must be usable in bright outdoor light (sufficient contrast), with one hand (touch targets min 44pt), and without network access (offline-first). Reduced motion: respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for all transitions.
